Pre-Clinical Animal Lab

Animal models have provided an important tool to help make the decision to take potential therapies from preclinical studies to humans. The use of animal models in research resulted in the advancement of knowledge for how diseases affect animals and humans. It has also led to the effective treatment of disease symptoms or in many cases disease cures. There are a wide variety of animal models used in drug discovery including, but not limited to mice, rats, rabbits, dogs, cats and non-human primates.

Creation of Pre-Clinical Models at PRF:

Prasad Research Foundation with its expert clinical and veterinary team involved in creation of pre-clinical models specially for lung, skin & hair.

  • Lung Model: Acute lung injury model
  • Skin & Hair: Complete skin excisional wound model
  • Toxicology model
  • Multiple Organ Dysfunction Syndrome (MODS) model
Clinical Research Organization

Also called as contract research organization (CRO), which is company that provides support to the pharmaceutical, biotechnology, and medical device industries in the form of research services outsourced on a contract basis. A CRO may provide such services as biopharmaceutical development, biologic assay development, commercialization, preclinical research, clinical research, clinical trials management, and pharmacovigilance.

Prasad Research Foundation is a site for external projects

  • Rare diseases registries sponsored by Sanofi India Private Limited.
  • Lyso-Gb1 biomarker in Gaucher disease patients sponsored by Centogene, Germany
  • Post marketing surveillance study for VPRIV (Velaglucerase alfa r-DNA) for Gaucher's disease sponsored by Shire/Takeda.

The CRU (Clinical Research Unit) functions as a Site Management Organization (SMO) that provides clinical trial related services to a contract research organization (CRO), a pharmaceutical company, a biotechnology company, a medical device company or investigators. Prasad Hospitals is the Clinical Research Unit (CRU) which provides site operations to Prasad Research Foundation for the above-mentioned studies.
