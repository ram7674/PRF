Our Vision & Mission

Our Vision

Our Vision is to pursue clinically relevant research on Regenerative proteome and transcriptomics with a potential therapeutic application in Dermatology and Pulmonology and orthopaedics. Improving mother and child health is our primary goal which involves a dynamic and continuous medical education program through visionary leaders.

Our Mission

Our mission is to involve clinicians and scientists from various disciplines in clinical research and apply knowledge to innovate novel technologies in biomarker development and regenerative medicine especially in the fields of mother and child health, pulmonology and orthopaedics.

Our Initial Projects

Skin and Hair Regeneration

Effect of aging and wounding are the major clinical reasons for the requirement of cosmeceutical therapy. The conventional management for skin rejuvenation and hair loss has been limited to chemical agents such as finasteride and minoxidil and allogenic platelet-rich plasma (PRP). However, their long-term side effects and difficulties in repetitive administration limit their effectiveness as standard therapies.

Prasad Research Foundation has developed a Protein and miRNA rich product, Su Charm®, derived from discarded fetal tissue, otherwise treated as biological waste post-birth. Su Charm® contains a potent blend of proteins, growth factors, and miRNAs—key components in regenerative medicine.

Su Charm® has been shown to promote Dermal Papilla Cell (DPC) regeneration and proliferation. Restoration of functional dermis and epidermis components, extracellular matrix (ECM), vasculogenesis, and cell migration were established through in-vitro and in-vivo mouse model studies. The underlying mechanisms are explained via protein signaling pathways and post-transcriptional regulation through miRNA.

Pulmonology: Anti-Fibrotic Therapy and Remodeling Following Injury

Prasad Research Foundation has made a breakthrough in lung regeneration for pulmonary fibrosis post-injury, demonstrated in both murine and non-rodent species models developed by our veterinary team under clinician guidance.

The regenerative process is facilitated by a cell-free, xeno-free clinical-grade proteome, ensuring safe and effective lung repair. Alveolar regeneration was completely established, highlighting the rejuvenating and regenerative properties of this unique proteome and juxtacrine signaling through a novel transcriptome.

A proteome, enzyme, and miRNA-rich product derived under strict cGMP conditions proves to be an efficient biological intervention for preventing and reversing lung fibrosis. This product is completely free from xenogenic components, ensuring its safety in human lung regeneration. It works via paracrine signaling, aiding in inflammation reduction, fibrosis reversal, and post-COVID lung recovery.

Role of Regenerative Medicine in Lung Regeneration & Alveolar Reconstruction

  • Regenerates all four lung cell types: epithelial, endothelial, immune, and mesenchymal cells.
  • Preserves lung lineage transcription factors crucial for fetal-derived tissue oxygen and nutrient transport.
  • Promotes vasculogenesis and angiogenesis, improving oxygen transport and microvascular permeability.
  • Enhances miR-21 expression, regulating immune cell maturation and monocyte differentiation.

This novel therapy is designed to combat inflammatory immune injuries and pulmonary fibrosis, offering benefits such as:

  • Availability of an on-the-shelf product.
  • Ease of storage, transport, and use in day-care settings.
  • Higher safety compared to stem cells (tumor risk) or monoclonal antibodies (immunosuppressive effects).
  • Intravenous administration with no reported adverse effects in pre-clinical studies.
  • More cost-effective than other biological therapies, making it accessible for treating lung fibrosis patients.
